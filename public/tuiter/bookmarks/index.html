Southern Maine home to hilltop views and salt marsh habitat Ted Reinstein hits Mount Agamenticus up high, and the Scarborough Marsh at sea level.


Mt. Agamenticus is a 692 ft. mountain in York, Maine. It’s also a “monadnock,” the word for a mountain that stands alone in an otherwise flat plain. A former ski area, today it’s a conservation area that is open to the public and is widely enjoyed for its hiking trails.

At more than 3,200 acres, Scarborough Marsh, in Scarborough, Maine, (Cumberland County) is the state’s largest saltwater estuary. It’s owned and managed by Maine’s Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife, who along with Maine Audubon conserves the saltwater marsh and keeps it open to public recreation for walking, hiking, bird-watching, canoeing and kayaking.
